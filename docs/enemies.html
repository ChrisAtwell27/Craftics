Enemy AI Behaviors

Every mob in Craftics has its own combat intelligence. 40+ mobs with 20+ unique AI strategies.

How AI Works

After the player ends their turn, each enemy acts in sequence. The AI evaluates four possible actions: move, attack, use a special ability, or flee. The decision depends on the mob's registered AI class, which encodes its personality and combat style.

Every mob type has a registered AI class that determines its behavior. When the AI's turn begins it checks line-of-sight, distance to the player, its own HP, and any active status effects before choosing what to do.

Mobs without a registered AI class default to PassiveAI, which means they wander or idle and flee when attacked.

Defense is percentage-based. Each point of defense reduces incoming damage by 5%, capped at 60%.

Default Mob Speeds

Mob speeds are defined in CombatEntity.java. Speed determines how many tiles a mob can move per turn. Any mob not listed below defaults to speed 2.

Biome Bosses

Each biome has a unique named boss encounter that serves as a progression milestone. Bosses telegraph their abilities with visual warnings, transition to a more aggressive Phase 2 at ≤50% HP, and reward a level-up on defeat.

Special Boss: The Warden

The Warden is the first major boss encounter, found in the Deep Dark biome. It hits incredibly hard with massive HP, high attack, and heavy defense.

  • Speed: 3. Range: 2.
  • Sonic Boom. A ranged attack that ignores defense entirely.
  • Extremely high HP pool, high attack damage, and heavy percentage-based defense.
  • Strategy: Defense-ignoring attacks like the Sonic Boom make the Warden lethal at any range. Burst damage and careful positioning are essential.

Final Boss: Ender Dragon

The Ender Dragon is the final boss of Craftics, fought in the Dragon's Nest. Defeating it triggers New Game+ (NG+).

  • Speed: 4.
  • Devastating Swoop. Hits everything in its path across the grid.
  • The ultimate test of everything you've learned. Extremely dangerous at all ranges.
  • Strategy: Learn the swoop lanes and stay mobile. Use your strongest abilities and items to close out the fight.

Trial Chambers

Trial Chambers are special combat events sourced from TrialChamberEvent.java. They come in four variants, each with different grid sizes, enemy counts, and loot tables.

Standard Trial

  • Grid size: 10-12 tiles.
  • Enemies: 4-6 mobs plus a Breeze mini-boss.
  • Loot tiers: Common, Uncommon, Rare, Epic, and Legendary.

Ambush

  • Grid size: 8×8.
  • Enemies: 2-3 weaker mobs.
  • Scaling: Gentler difficulty scaling. A lighter encounter.

Ominous Trial

  • Grid size: 12-14 tiles.
  • Enemies: 5-7 mobs plus a Breeze and a Warden.
  • Scaling: Harder difficulty scaling than Standard.
  • Loot: Better rewards including Netherite gear, Mace, and other rare items.

Treasure Vault

  • Grid size: 5×5.
  • Enemies: None. Automatic victory.
  • Loot: 3-5 items. A free reward room.
