Usable Items

Every item you can use in combat, grouped by the damage affinity it scales with. Affinity decides whether your invested points make an item hit harder, so it is the main thing worth knowing about a consumable.

How Affinity Works

There are eight damage types. Each has an affinity track fed by level-ups, armor, trims and mob heads. Every affinity point is worth +3 damage of that type.

Weapons get their type from the weapon registry. Usable items do not: each item's handler states its own type when it deals damage. That means an item either names a type and scales with your affinity, or names nothing and deals raw damage that no investment improves.

Most combat consumables are Special. Sherds, potions, horns and most throwables all scale with Special affinity, which makes Special the single most valuable track for an item-focused build. The exceptions are listed below and they are worth knowing, because points spent elsewhere do nothing for them.

Goat Horns Special

Reusable battle cries. The horn is never consumed, so you only pay AP. Re-casting a buff horn raises its level by one, up to level IV, and keeps the longer duration.

Every horn costs 5 AP. That is more than a base turn's 3 AP, so a horn is a setup tool: you spend a whole turn on it and need AP from your stats, a trim, a set bonus or Haste to afford one at all. You cannot chain several in a turn. The horn itself is never consumed, so AP is the only thing limiting it.

The Special Conserve Perk

Every point of Special affinity gives a 10% chance not to consume the item you just used. At 5 points that is half your consumables surviving use, which is the real reason a sherd build invests in Special beyond the damage.

Applies to: all three potion kinds, goat horns, fire and wind charges, snowballs, eggs, ender pearls, banners and pottery sherds. Anvils have their own separate roll at the same odds.

Does not apply to: TNT, hay blocks, pickaxes and bricks. These are always consumed.
