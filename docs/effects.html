Status Effects

Every status effect in Craftics, for players and enemies. Players and enemies use separate effect systems, so several effects behave differently depending on who has them.

How Effects Work

Craftics effects are turn-based, not tick-based. They count down once per turn instead of running on a real-time clock, and they are tracked separately from vanilla Minecraft's effect system.

Every effect has a level. Most formulas below scale with it, so an effect listed as "+2 movement/level" gives +2 at level I and +6 at level III.

RuleBehavior
Duration cap10 turns by default, set by maxCombatEffectDuration. Any longer duration is clamped down to it.
Re-applyingReplaces the effect outright. A new application overwrites both the duration and the level, so re-applying a weaker version downgrades a stronger one.
Frozen effectsApplied in the hub, they sit inert with no countdown until combat begins. While frozen, an effect does nothing at all.
Water beats fireBurning cannot be applied while Soaked, and applying Soaked removes Burning outright.
Damage over timePlayer DoT can never kill you. It always leaves at least 1 HP.

The remaining four debuffs deal damage over time and are covered below.

Damage Over Time

Four effects deal damage at the start of your turn. They behave very differently from each other, and three of them are made worse by the attacker's Special affinity.

EffectDamage per turnShape
Poison (2 x level) + turns remaining + Special affinity Front-loaded. Hits hardest on the first tick and weakens as it runs out, because the turns-remaining term shrinks.
Wither (1 + level + Special affinity) x turns elapsed Ramping. The opposite of Poison. Starts small and climbs the longer it runs, so the last tick is the worst.
Burning 1 + level + Special affinity Flat. Same every turn. Blocked entirely by Fire Resistance.
Bleeding stacks x (stacks + 1) / 2 Triangular. 1, 3, 6, 10, 15 as stacks climb. Not affected by Special affinity. Bleed is its own damage source and adds nothing to direct hits.

Poison and Wither are mirror images. Poison front-loads and fades, so cleansing it late saves you little. Wither ramps, so cleansing it late saves you the most. Bleeding punishes stacking rather than duration: four stacks is 10 damage a turn, not 4.

Enemy Effects

Enemies use a completely separate system from players. Several effects share a name with a player effect but use different math, so the same effect is not always the same thing.

Every enemy damage-over-time effect adds a max HP bonus of max(1, maxHP / 20) on top of the listed formula. That makes DoT scale into big-health targets and bosses instead of falling off.

Cleansing

A cleanse removes the twelve debuffs and leaves buffs alone. A Milk Bucket is different: it clears everything, good and bad.

SourceAPRemoves
Milk Bucket3All effects, buffs included. Works on yourself or an adjacent teammate.
Cleanse (instrument signature)VariesDebuffs only, on all friendlies.
Fire Resistance-Prevents Burning rather than removing it.
Soaked-Removes Burning and prevents it from being re-applied.
